HISTORY

The Game of Life is a cellular automaton devised by the British mathematician John Horton Conway in 1970.

It's a zero-player game, meaning that its evolution is determined by its initial state, requiring no further input.

One interacts with the Game of Life by creating an initial configuration and observing how it evolves or, for advanced players, by creating patterns with particular properties.

CONCEPT

CONCEPT

The universe of the Game of Life is an infinite two-dimensional grid of cells, each of which is in one of two possible states, alive or dead.

Every cell interacts with its eight neighbours, which are the cells that are horizontally, vertically, or diagonally adjacent. At each generations (step in time), some following rules are applied...

RULES

RULES

-Any live cell with fewer than two live neighbours dies, as if caused by under-population.
-Any live cell with two or three live neighbours lives on to the next generation.
-Any live cell with more than three live neighbours dies, as if by overcrowding.
-Any dead cell with exactly three live neighbours becomes a live cell, as if by reproduction.
(This rule is called "B3/S23" for 3 -> Born, 2 or 3 -> Survive, some variante existe...)
